Glimpse I

Quiet corners, long afternoons.

It started on the floor: linen on the ground, a few wooden pieces, the sound of tiny wheels and pages turning. No hurry, no schedule — just the slow rhythm of a child discovering the same objects again and again.

Nothing to buy. Nothing to launch. Just notes.
Glimpse II

Between here and somewhere familiar.

Some little ones fall asleep to one language and wake up to another. We’re sketching quiet ways for them to trace back where they come from, without turning it into homework or a performance.

play, place, and gentle belonging
Glimpse III

Small journeys, soft adventures.

Wheels, windows, tiny drivers and unmarked roads. We’re less interested in where these vehicles are going, and more in the conversations that happen while a child quietly drives them there.
